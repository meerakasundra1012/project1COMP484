About the Harry Potter Series

The seven-book series follows Harry Potter from age eleven to seventeen as he grows from a boy living under the stairs to the hero who defeats Lord Voldemort. Written by J.K. Rowling, the books have sold over 500 million copies worldwide and have been translated into dozens of languages.

Publication Timeline

  • 1997 Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone

    Harry's first year at Hogwarts. The book was rejected by several publishers before Bloomsbury accepted it.

  • 1998 Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets

    Harry returns for his second year and uncovers the mystery of the Chamber of Secrets.

  • 1999 Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban

    Sirius Black escapes Azkaban; Harry learns the truth about his parents and their friends.

  • 2000 Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire

    The Triwizard Tournament brings danger and the return of Voldemort.

  • 2003 Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix

    Harry forms Dumbledore's Army and faces loss at the Department of Mysteries.

  • 2005 Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince

    Voldemort's past is revealed; Hogwarts loses its greatest protector.

  • 2007 Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows

    The final book brings the saga to an epic conclusion with the Battle of Hogwarts.

Fun Facts

  • J.K. Rowling wrote much of the first book in cafés in Edinburgh while raising her daughter as a single parent.
  • The series was originally published as "Philosopher's Stone" in the UK and "Sorcerer's Stone" in the US.
  • Each book corresponds to one school year at Hogwarts, so Harry ages from 11 to 17 across the series.
  • Rowling had the entire plot planned out before finishing the first book—including the fate of major characters.
  • Hogwarts has four houses: Gryffindor, Hufflepuff, Ravenclaw, and Slytherin, each with distinct values and colors.
